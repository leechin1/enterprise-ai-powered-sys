1# Misty Jazz Records - Vinyl Grading Guide

## Introduction

Accurate grading is essential to maintaining customer trust and ensuring fair pricing. This guide establishes our standards for evaluating vinyl record condition.

---

## Grading Scale

### M (Mint) - 10/10
**Definition**: Perfect in every way. Never played, possibly still sealed.

**Vinyl Disc**:
- Absolutely pristine
- No marks, scratches, or scuffs
- Perfect gloss
- Never been on a turntable

**Album Cover**:
- No ring wear, creases, or marks
- Perfect corners
- Original shrink wrap (if sealed)
- All inserts mint and present

**Rarity**: Extremely rare for vintage records
**Price Impact**: 100% of Discogs maximum

---

### NM (Near Mint) - 9/10
**Definition**: Looks and plays like new. Might have been played once or twice with extreme care.

**Vinyl Disc**:
- Brilliant gloss
- No visible marks under normal light
- Possible light paper scuffs (no audio impact)
- Clean, crisp sound

**Album Cover**:
- Minimal shelf wear
- Sharp corners
- No seam splits
- Minor ring wear acceptable
- All inserts clean and present

**Usage**: Most common grade for well-cared-for records
**Price Impact**: 85-95% of Discogs maximum

---

### VG+ (Very Good Plus) - 7-8/10
**Definition**: Shows light use but still excellent for listening.

**Vinyl Disc**:
- Light surface marks visible
- Possible light scratches (not felt with fingernail)
- Might have light background noise
- No skips or major distortion
- Overall clean sound

**Album Cover**:
- Some ring wear
- Light edge wear
- Small creases acceptable
- Minor seam wear (not split)
- Writing/stickers reduce grade
- May be missing inserts

**Usage**: Most common grade for vintage jazz records
**Price Impact**: 60-75% of Discogs maximum

---

### VG (Very Good) - 5-6/10
**Definition**: Shows obvious signs of use but still a good playing copy.

**Vinyl Disc**:
- Visible scratches and scuffs
- Light scratches felt with fingernail
- Surface noise present
- Possible light clicks/pops
- No skipping on proper setup
- Music still clear and enjoyable

**Album Cover**:
- Moderate ring wear
- Edge wear and splitting at one seam
- Several creases
- Corner wear
- Possible tape repairs
- May have writing or stickers
- Missing inserts noted

**Usage**: Acceptable for listeners, not collectors
**Price Impact**: 30-50% of Discogs maximum

---

### G+ (Good Plus) - 4/10
**Definition**: Well-played copy with significant wear.

**Vinyl Disc**:
- Many visible scratches
- Noticeable surface noise throughout
- Clicks and pops present
- Might have light skips
- Music still discernible
- Play-through possible

**Album Cover**:
- Heavy ring wear
- Multiple seam splits
- Significant edge wear
- Creases and tears
- Water damage possible
- Heavy writing/stickers
- Missing inserts common

**Usage**: Placeholder until better copy found
**Price Impact**: 15-25% of Discogs maximum

---

### G (Good) - 3/10
**Definition**: Very worn but still plays.

**Vinyl Disc**:
- Heavy scratches and scuffs
- Constant surface noise
- Skips likely on some tracks
- Distortion possible
- Music recognizable
- Playable with patience

**Album Cover**:
- Severely worn
- Water damage
- Missing chunks
- Heavy tape repairs
- Split seams
- May be generic sleeve

**Usage**: Last resort for rare titles
**Price Impact**: 10-15% of Discogs maximum

---

### P (Poor) - 1-2/10
**Definition**: Barely playable or unplayable.

**Vinyl Disc**:
- Extremely scratched
- Heavy warping
- Skips extensively
- Severe damage
- For parts/decoration only

**Album Cover**:
- Destroyed condition
- May be absent

**Usage**: Rarely sold, parts only
**Price Impact**: <10% of Discogs maximum

---

## Special Considerations

### Plus (+) and Minus (-)
- **VG+**: Better than VG, not quite NM
- **VG-**: Worse than VG, better than G+
- Use sparingly and only when between grades

### Grading Components Separately

**Format**: `Vinyl: NM / Cover: VG+`

Always grade vinyl and cover separately as they often differ in condition.

## Common Defects & Terminology

### Vinyl Disc Issues

**Warping**
- **Minor**: Plays fine, visible warp
- **Moderate**: Audible wow/flutter
- **Severe**: Unplayable, tonearm jumps

**Surface Noise**
- **Crackle**: Random pops and clicks
- **Hiss**: Constant low-level noise
- **Groove Wear**: Loss of high frequencies

**Pressing Defects**
- **Off-Center**: Hole not perfectly centered (causes wow)
- **Non-Fill**: Portions not fully pressed
- **Dish Warp**: Slight bowl shape (common, usually plays fine)

**Damage**
- **Scratches**: Light (superficial) vs. Deep (audible)
- **Scuffs**: Friction marks from sliding (may not affect sound)
- **Spindle Marks**: Circles around label (cosmetic)

### Cover Issues

**Ring Wear**: Circular impression from vinyl edge
- Light, moderate, or heavy

**Seam Splits**: Opening at edges
- Measure in inches (e.g., "1-inch split at top")

**Corner Wear**:
- **Dinged**: Slightly bumped
- **Crushed**: Flattened corner
- **Cut**: Corner bent back

**Water Damage**: Warping, staining, mold
- Always disclose

**Writing**: Pen/marker on cover
- Note location and size

**Stickers**: Price tags, promotional stickers
- Note if removable or permanent

**Punch Holes**: Cutout/promotional copies
- Always disclose

## Grading Procedure

### Step 1: Initial Inspection
1. Check for obvious damage
2. Note any immediate red flags
3. Verify pressing details match description

### Step 2: Vinyl Examination
1. **Visual**: Examine under bright light
2. **Physical**: Feel surface gently for deep scratches
3. **Play Testing**: Play first 30 seconds of each side (when possible)
4. **Spindle**: Check for warping

### Step 3: Cover Assessment
1. **Front**: Check artwork, corners, edges
2. **Back**: Check seams, damage
3. **Spine**: Check splits, wear
4. **Inside**: Check gatefold (if applicable)

### Step 4: Inserts & Extras
1. Verify all listed components present
2. Check condition of inserts
3. Note any missing items

### Step 5: Documentation
1. Assign grade for vinyl
2. Assign grade for cover
3. Note specific defects
4. Add detailed notes

## Grading Examples

### Example 1: Blue Note Classic
**Title**: John Coltrane - Blue Train (Blue Note 1577)
**Pressing**: Original 1958, RVG stamp, NY label

**Grade**: Vinyl: VG+ / Cover: VG

**Description**:
"Original 1958 Blue Note pressing in very good plus condition. Vinyl shows light surface marks and some light scratches that don't affect playback significantly. Plays with minimal surface noise, no skips. Iconic cover has moderate ring wear, 1-inch split at top seam, and some edge wear. All original insert intact. A solid playing copy of this essential hard bop classic."

## Honest Grading Philosophy

### Always Grade Conservatively
- **When in doubt**: Grade lower
- **Customer trust**: Worth more than $5
- **Better surprise**: "Better than expected" reviews

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- **Overgrading**: Leads to returns and bad reviews
- **Ignoring Playback**: Visual inspection isn't enough
- **Missing Defects**: Check entire surface
- **Assuming**: Verify pressing details

### Disclosure is Key
- **Always mention**: Skips, major scratches, water damage
- **Photo evidence**: For valuable or problematic items
- **Specific details**: "Light scratch on track 3, side A"

## Tools & Equipment

### Required
- **Turntable**: For play testing
- **Bright Light**: LED or halogen for inspection
- **Clean Surface**: White or light-colored examination table
- **Cleaning Supplies**: Microfiber cloths, cleaning solution

### Recommended
- **Magnifying Glass**: For close inspection
- **Gram Scale**: Check vinyl weight
- **Calipers**: Measure seam splits
- **Camera**: Document defects

## Pricing by Grade

### General Formula
- **NM**: 85-95% of Discogs high
- **VG+**: 60-75% of Discogs high
- **VG**: 30-50% of Discogs high
- **G+**: 15-25% of Discogs high
- **G or lower**: Case-by-case, usually <$10

### Adjustments
- **Rare titles**: Add 10-20%
- **First pressings**: Add 15-30%
- **Audiophile labels**: Add 10-25%
- **Common titles**: Subtract 10-20%
